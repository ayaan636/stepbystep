About Mongolia

Mongolia is its own world of endless blue sky green steppes rolling to the horizon and unbroken deep silence the herders invite you in for a mug of steaming hot milk tea and yarn ball spin by the campfire. There are star-filled skies at night outside far and pristine from city light It's nature at its stark, pure best, as great as it is.

How To Get There

It runs Abu Dhabi (AUH) or Dubai (DXB) to Ulaanbaatar (UBN) flights with intermediate stops in Beijing, Seoul, or Istanbul. The principal carriers of the flights are Turkish Airlines and MIAT Mongolian Airlines. The travelers are carried through the open Mongolian landscape in 4x4 transport and air travel.

When To Go There

Summer (June-August) for hot weather, green landscapes, and the Naadam Festival. Spring and autumn are peaceful and picturesque winter (November-February) is cold but magical with white steppes and the opportunity to visit Mongolia's Ice Festival.

Things To See There

Horse ride through boundless steppes, overnight stay in a ger in an authentic yurt, stroll along the dunes of the Gobi Desert, and monasteries in Ulaanbaatar witness eagle hunters in the Altai Mountains, soak in hot springs and gaze up at the infinite, unspoiled Mongolian sky.
